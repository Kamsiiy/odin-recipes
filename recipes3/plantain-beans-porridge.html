Plantain Beans Porridge

Description

Plantain and beans porridge is a savory and slightly sweet dish made by cooking beans and ripe plantains together. The beans are typically black-eyed peas or honey beans, which are simmered until tender. Ripe plantains are then added, along with onions, crayfish (optional), stock cubes, red palm oil, salt, and pepper. The result is a rich, flavorful porridge with a creamy texture and a vibrant color from the palm oil.

Ingredients to make Plantain Beans Porridge

  • 4 cups of Honey beans/Nigeria brown beans/Black eyed peas
  • 1/4 cup of palm oil
  • 4 Roma Tomatoes
  • 4 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 large red onion
  • 1 large bell pepper
  • 5 tbsp Ground dried shrimp(aka crayfish) optional
  • 3 bouillon cubes
  • Salt to taste

Steps in making Plantain Beans Porridge

  1. Rinse the beans and place in a deep pot with 7 cups of water.
  2. Boil the beans on medium heat until it becomes soft, it could take about 40 mins. If you are using black eyed peas, it might take a little longer.
  3. While the beans are boiling, blend the tomatoes, peppers, and onions and set aside
  4. Once the beans are softened from boiling (do not drain the water), turn the heat down to low, the pour in the blended tomatoes into pot.
  5. Allow the beans to cook for an additional 10 minutes with the tomatoes, then add in the crayfish, bullion and palm oil. Stir, and cook for another 10 minutes.
  6. Season the beans with salt to your taste. Turn off the heat, and allow to sit for 5 minutes before serving
